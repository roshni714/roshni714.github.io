I am an assistant professor in the [Decision, Risk, and Operations](https://business.columbia.edu/faculty/divisions/dro) Division at [Columbia Business School](https://business.columbia.edu/). I work on data-driven decision making for social impact. My research develops statistical methodology to address challenges in [public health](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.14858) and [economic development](https://www.nber.org/papers/w34583), combining tools from machine learning, causal inference, and optimization. 

{:.content-narrow .content-block}
I completed my PhD in Computer Science at Stanford University. Prior to my doctoral studies, I received Bachelor of Science degrees in Computer Science and Mathematics at MIT.

{:.content-narrow .content-block}
Outside of research, I love to practice yoga, run, swim, and read.

{:.content-narrow .content-block}
